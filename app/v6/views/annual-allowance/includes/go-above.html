Changes to your allowance

You can increase your annual allowance by including any unused annual allowance from the previous three years. This is called carry forward.

If your income is over £200,000 your annual allowance in the same tax year will be reduced. This is called tapering.

The income threshold changed from £110,000 to £200,000 in March 2020.

If you go above the allowance

You’ll only pay tax if your total savings across all your pensions go above the annual allowance.

If you have a tax charge you can pay directly to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) yourself or share the responsibility with us by using scheme pays.

You can use a calculator on GOV.UK to work out if you have to pay tax on your pension savings.

You might want to consider getting financial help.
