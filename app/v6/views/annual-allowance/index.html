Your annual allowance is the most you can save in your pension pots in a tax year (6 April to 5 April) before you have to pay tax.

You’ll only pay tax if you go above the annual allowance. This is £40,000 this tax year.

Find out more about annual allowance on GOV.UK.

How is my statement calculated?

The values shown are based on your pay information and membership record from your employer up to {{ data['lastUpdatedDate'] }}. It is up to your employer to keep your pension information up to date.

If you go above the allowance

If your savings are higher than the allowance, you are likely to have a tax charge. You can use a calculator on GOV.UK to work out how much you have gone above the allowance.

What's next?

If you have a tax advisor you may wish to send them a detailed statement so they can consider any tax charges for you.

See the annual allowance section on our website to help you understand what to do next.
